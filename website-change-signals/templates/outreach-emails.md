# Outreach Templates — Website Change Signals

Copy-paste templates for when you detect a website change.

---

## Template 1: Target Company Rebranded

**Use when:** A company you want to sell to just rebranded their site.

**Subject options:**
- Saw the rebrand — looks sharp
- The new site looks great
- Quick note on the rebrand

**Body:**

```
Hey [First Name],

Noticed [Company] just refreshed the site — the new positioning around [what you noticed] is solid.

When companies go through rebrands, they're usually rethinking a lot of other things too. If [your service area] is on the list, happy to share what's working for similar companies right now.

Either way, congrats on the launch.

[Your signature]
```

---

## Template 2: Competitor Rebranded (Target Their Customers)

**Use when:** A competitor just rebranded and you want to reach their customers.

**Subject options:**
- Quick question about [Competitor]
- Noticed [Competitor] changed things up
- Curious about your experience with [Competitor]

**Body:**

```
Hey [First Name],

Saw [Competitor] just went through a major rebrand — usually means some changes under the hood too.

If you've noticed any disruption or are just curious what else is out there, happy to show you what we're doing differently.

No pressure either way.

[Your signature]
```

**Use when:** Company has new CMO/VP Marketing AND their site changed.

**Subject:** Congrats on the new role + the rebrand

**Body:**

```

New marketing leaders usually want to put quick wins on the board. If [your service area] is something you're evaluating, happy to share what's working for companies at your stage.

Either way, good luck with the new gig.

[Your signature]
```

**Use when:** You want a lighter touch, just opening a conversation.

**Subject:** The new [Company] site

**Body:**

```
Hey [First Name],

Just saw the new [Company] site — solid refresh.

[Your signature]
```

---

## Personalization Tips

1. **Be specific:** Mention what actually changed (new tagline, new colors, new messaging)

2. **Don't be creepy:** "Noticed you updated your site" is fine. "I've been watching your site every day for 3 months" is not.

3. **Connect to your offer:** Tie the change to something you can help with.

4. **Timing matters:** Reach out within 1-2 weeks of the change, not months later.

---

## Subject Line A/B Tests

**Higher open rates:**
- "The new site looks great"
- "Quick question about the rebrand"
- "Saw the changes"

**Lower open rates:**
- "Noticed your website changed" (too surveillance-y)
- "Congrats on the rebrand!" (too salesy)
